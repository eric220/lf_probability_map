Abstract:
This project is a cooperative effort between NOAA's National Centers for Coastal Ocean Science (NCCOS), U.S. Geological Survey, National Park Service, and the National Geophysical Data Center, to produce benthic habitat maps and georeferenced imagery for Puerto Rico. This project was conducted in support of the U.S. Coral Reef Task Force.

Time_Period_of_Content:
Currentness_Reference:
Ground condition at date photos were taken (Feb, Mar, and Dec 1999). It should be noted that this season is in general dominated by more energetic sea conditions. This results in temporary erosion of beaches and other coastal features. Eroded beaches often experience accretion of sand during less energetic seasons. Maps reflect only the conditions at the time of the photos.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Ground control points, camera calibration data, and USGS DTM data were used to orthorectify 500 dpi scans of the aerial photos using Socet Set Version 4.2.1. Orthorectified images were then loaded into ArcView 3.2 with the Image Analyst and Habitat Digitizer Extensions activated. This allowed the photointerpreter to interpret features, delineate and attribute benthic habitat directly into a digital reference system. The data were saved as ArcView shapefiles.
